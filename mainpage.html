Information

Abortion

Abortion is a medical procedure that ends pregnancy by removing the embryo or fetus from the uterus. It can be done using medication or surgery and is a complex and personal decision influenced by factors like individual circumstances, health, and personal beliefs.

IVF

IVF, or in vitro fertilization, is a fertility treatment where eggs are retrieved from a woman's ovaries and combined with sperm in a laboratory. The fertilized embryos are then transferred back into the woman's uterus with the aim of achieving a successful pregnancy.

Contraceptive

Contraceptives are methods used to prevent pregnancy by either blocking the sperm from reaching the egg or by inhibiting the release of eggs. They can be barrier methods like condoms, hormonal methods like birth control pills, or intrauterine devices (IUDs) that prevent implantation.

Plan B

Plan B, also known as the "morning-after pill" or emergency contraception, is a type of medication used to prevent pregnancy after unprotected sex or contraceptive failure. Plan B is most effective when taken as soon as possible after intercourse, but it should not be considered a regular form of contraception and should be used as a backup method in emergency situations.

Menstrual Products

Menstrual products are items designed to manage menstrual flow during a woman's menstrual cycle. They include options such as sanitary pads, tampons, menstrual cups, and period underwear. These products are used to absorb or collect menstrual blood, providing comfort and hygiene while allowing women to continue their daily activities during menstruation.
